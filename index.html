How it works

Payout and settlement move on separate but controlled tracks.

Aracore coordinates the state transitions that let institutions pay locally after lock verification while settling later through a controlled net settlement cycle.

1. Quote and instruction

The sending bank creates a transaction, requests a quote, and sends the payment instruction through Aracore messaging.

2. Reference and lock

A Settlement Reference is fixed and transaction-level lock status is verified before payout is allowed to progress.

3. Local payout

The receiving bank pays the beneficiary locally and returns credit confirmation to the orchestration layer.

4. Settlement object

Aracore tracks payout, evidence, exception state, and settlement objects across the corridor workflow.

5. Net demand

Unsettled transactions are aggregated into settlement demand so institutions can reduce cross-border liquidity movement.

6. Completion and release

Settlement completion triggers final state updates and release of locked assets according to the corridor rules.
